# What is a Perpetual Swap in Crypto Trading?

**Perpetual swaps (or "perpetuals") are a type of crypto derivative contract that allows traders to speculate on an asset's future price without an expiration date.** 

Unlike traditional futures contracts that settle on a specific calendar date, you can hold a perpetual position open indefinitely, provided you maintain enough collateral to cover your trades.

---

## Core Mechanics of Perpetuals

* **No Expiration Date:** You can keep trades open for days, months, or years.
* **Leverage:** Traders can borrow funds to open positions much larger than their account balance.
* **Long and Short Positions:** You can profit from rising prices (long) or falling prices (short).
* **Cash Settlement:** No actual crypto is delivered; profits and losses settle in stablecoins or the base crypto.

---

## How the Price Stays Anchored (Funding Rate)

Because perpetuals never expire, exchanges use a mechanism called the **Funding Rate** to ensure the contract price stays close to the actual spot market price of the cryptocurrency.

* **Payments Every Few Hours:** Buyers (longs) and sellers (shorts) pay each other directly, usually every 1 to 8 hours.
* **When Contract Price > Spot Price:** The funding rate is positive. Longs must pay shorts. This incentivizes selling, pushing the contract price down.
* **When Contract Price < Spot Price:** The funding rate is negative. Shorts must pay longs. This incentivizes buying, pushing the contract price up.

---

## Key Risks to Consider

* **Liquidation Risk:** High leverage drastically shrinks your room for error. If the market moves against you and your account balance drops below the maintenance margin, the exchange will forcefully close your position and you will lose your collateral.
* **Funding Fees:** If you hold a position for a long time against prevailing market momentum, funding fees can heavily drain your account balance.
* **Extreme Volatility:** Crypto markets move fast. Sharp, sudden price swings can trigger massive cascades of liquidations.
