# 📈 Crypto Market Makers vs. Takers

In crypto, **Makers** add liquidity to an exchange by placing orders that sit on the order book waiting to be filled. **Takers** remove liquidity by buying or selling immediately against those resting orders. Because makers provide market stability, exchanges usually charge them lower fees than takers.

> Understanding exactly how these two types of trades operate is the best way to optimize your trading costs.

---

## 1. Market Makers

### What They Do
Makers *"make"* the market by placing **Limit Orders** (e.g., buying Bitcoin only if it drops to $60,000) that do not execute instantly. Instead, the order is stored in the exchange's **order book**.

### Why They Are Rewarded
They bring supply and demand into the market, providing the *liquidity* necessary for others to trade smoothly.

### The Fees
Because they add value to the exchange, maker fees are generally **lower** — and on some platforms, they can even earn a small **rebate**.

---

## 2. Market Takers

### What They Do
Takers *"take"* liquidity out of the market by filling existing orders. They primarily use **Market Orders** to buy or sell instantly at the best available price.

### Why They Pay More
Takers utilize the liquidity and convenience provided by the makers to execute their trades immediately.

### The Fees
Because the trade executes right away and removes a resting order from the order book, taker fees are typically **higher**.

---

## 🌍 Real-World Example

Imagine you want to buy Ethereum:

- **You set a limit order** to buy at a price lower than the current market → your order enters the Order Book → you are acting as a **Maker**.
- **Someone else sells immediately** at the current best price → their trade matches with your resting order → they are acting as the **Taker**.

---

## 📊 Summary of Differences

| Feature | Maker | Taker |
|---|---|---|
| **Order Type** | Limit Orders | Market Orders (primarily) |
| **Immediacy** | Waits to be filled | Fills immediately |
| **Liquidity** | Adds to the order book | Removes from the order book |
| **Fees** | Lower fees or a rebate | Higher fees for convenience |
